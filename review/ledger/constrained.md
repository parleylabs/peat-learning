## Systemic findings

1. **No status labeling.** The track has zero shipped/in-flight/proposed/speculative markers. The DoD (§11) requires every claim labeled. This is the single biggest gap and affects ~every row above.
2. **SBD/LoRa treated as real throughout.** The track's entire spine (PACE ladder, anti-entropy-per-satellite-pass, summary-up/commands-down, cost-cliff topology) is built on transports that are **ADR proposals with no crate, no module, no impl** (peat.md §1; peat-mesh.md §1; confirmed absent in peat-btle/peat-lite/peat-node). Must be relabeled Proposed/Speculative.
3. **"NodeId = 256-bit" is the most embarrassing single error** a skeptical reader catches in the first body paragraph: the real DeviceId is 128-bit (SHA-256 truncated to 16 bytes), and lite/btle NodeId is a u32.
4. **`command_log` presented as a near-term collection config option** (cn11 YAML) when it exists nowhere — Speculative, not even a design.
5. **Anti-entropy digest scheme (version-vector / snapshot-since-T / hierarchical digest / IBLT)** is presented as PEAT's mechanism; the shipped mechanism is negentropy + Automerge sync over QUIC. The digest scheme is teaching-only design.
6. **Gateway role conflation.** The track's "gateway 🗼" does data-plane merge/translate/relay; the actual `peat-gateway` product is a control plane that does none of that. Rename to "hub/relay/bridge node."
7. **Issue #857 mis-cited** for tombstone/deletion GC; #857 is ADR-046 Phase-4 selectors.
8. **FIPS framing outdated.** Shipped peat-btle/peat-lite are FIPS-clean (AES-256-GCM/ECDH-P256, or no symmetric crypto at all). The ChaCha20 conflict survives only in **Proposed ADR-052 (LoRa)** and in stale READMEs — not in shipped constrained transports.
9. **Quantitative figures** (BLE 2 Mbps/100–400 m, LoRa 7–87 km/1.5–9.1 kB/s, SBD 1,960 B/5–20 s/$0.04–0.13, Automerge ~10 MB vs 256 KB, "~40×", "~40 s/two paid msgs") are all external hardware specs or unverified estimates — none are PEAT measurements. Must be sourced/flagged.
